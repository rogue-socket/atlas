# ETR ee=0.75 — 3-of-3 sweep (symmetric to cc=0.85)

> **Branch:** `feature/etr-cross-doc` @ `1ecd7f7`.
> **Pairs with:** `audits/2026-05-19_etr-per-kind-3of3-sweep.md` (cc=0.85 sweep that closes flat 0.80 as the cc default).
> **Corpus:** `test_proj`, same warm cache + post-extract graph as the cc=0.85 sweep.
> **Backend:** Gemini 2.5 Flash adjudication, `gemini-embedding-2-preview` (3072-dim), new project Gemini API key.
> **Cost:** ~$0.10–0.15 / run × 3 runs ≈ $0.40 (band is ~3× the baseline, ~9 batches each).
>
> **TL;DR:** Loosening `entityEntity` floor to 0.75 grew the ee adjudication band from ~17 → ~120 pairs/run and produced **6/6/6 approvals** (Flash became markedly more stable with the longer prompt). Stable intersection rose 3 → 4 vs baseline; 2 pairs from the new 0.75–0.80 ee band entered the stable set. Both gained pairs warrant rubric grading before any default change — surface read suggests one likely false-positive (principle↔regulation) and one borderline (paraphrase or different-scope). Net: per-kind ee loosening is *plausibly* a recall win on this corpus, but precision is unverified without the rubric.

## Method

3 `--etr-only` runs with `--adj-floor 0.80 --adj-floor-ee 0.75` against the same warm-cache project graph used for yesterday's cc=0.85 sweep. Baseline reused (`07-22-19Z`, `07-28-59Z`, `07-29-58Z` from earlier today). The 3 ee=0.75 sidecars (`08-37-14Z`, `08-40-23Z`, `08-43-09Z`) all confirm `adjudicationFloorPerKind: { entityEntity: 0.75 }` in their JSON.

The ee band expanded ~7× (≈17 → ≈121 pairs). cc and cl bands held roughly constant (cc=31 vs baseline 33–36 — minor embedding-noise drift around the 0.80 boundary).

Notable: per-run approvals are **dramatically more stable** under ee=0.75 (6/6/6) than under baseline (12/8/7) or cc=0.85 (3/1/1). Hypothesis: a longer adjudication prompt (more pairs per batch) shifts Flash's default toward conservative rejection, which suppresses the per-run noise that drives the baseline's 12 → 7 swing. Cost is the lower average approval count — but the stable set actually grew.

## Rubric grading the gained pairs

Source-text grading from the per-doc graph payloads (compliance / clinical / org PDFs decoded out of `StoredGraph.payload`):

### Pair 1 — `Data serves the patient ↔ Patient Rights Under HIPAA` (0.767) → **FALSE POSITIVE**

| Side | Label | Source (doc/page) | Summary / textSpan |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | `Data serves the patient` | org_and_people p.0 | *"Patients own their data and can export, share, or delete it on demand."* |
| B | `Patient Rights Under HIPAA` | compliance p.0 | *"Specific rights granted to patients regarding their medical records and protected health information under HIPAA."* |

LHS is a VitaCare-specific commitment (a principle / brand value: patient data ownership + portability). RHS is a regulatory framework defining a federal floor of patient rights. They overlap on the topic "patient access to their data" but are not the same thing — VitaCare's commitment can include rights *above* the HIPAA floor (instant delete-on-demand is not a HIPAA right), and HIPAA covers many rights beyond data ownership (notice of privacy practices, accounting of disclosures, etc.). Fits v4's "Parallel regimes that share a noun" KEEP-SEPARATE anti-pattern. Flash missed it.

### Pair 2 — `Workforce Access ↔ additional access controls` (0.766) → **BORDERLINE, lean KEEP-SEPARATE**

| Side | Label | Source (doc/page) | Summary / textSpan |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | `Workforce Access` | compliance p.1 | *"Access to Part 2 records is limited to clinicians and support staff with a defined treatment role, with access events logged and reviewed."* |
| B | `additional access controls` | clinical p.3 | *"Behavioral health records are subject to extra access controls within the VitaCare clinical system."* |

LHS is a workforce / role-based access policy with logging; RHS is technical controls in the clinical-system layer. The record sets overlap (Part 2 SUD records ⊂ broader BH records, both stricter than baseline PHI), but the controls are at *different layers* of governance: people-policy vs technical-controls. Fits v4's "Object ↔ property of object" KEEP-SEPARATE pattern. Could conceivably be argued the other way (both are "extra access controls on regulated records") but the operational pattern they describe is distinct.

### Rubric verdict

- 0 of 2 gained pairs are clear MERGE.
- 1 clear FP, 1 borderline that leans FP.
- The 1 stable approval lost (`Continuity matters ↔ Primary Care Model`) was confirmed earlier as Flash noise — not a structural threshold effect from ee=0.75.

**ee=0.75 is net-zero-to-negative on precision for this corpus.** The new band exposes pairs that v4's prompt doesn't reliably filter (and may not even be designed to filter — the "parallel regimes" anti-pattern targets cc-level cases, not entity-entity ones).

## Verdict

ee=0.75 surfaces real new candidates but they're dominated by **false-positives that v4's prompt is not designed to filter at the entity-entity level**. The 2 stable gains both fit cc-level KEEP-SEPARATE patterns ("parallel regimes that share a noun", "object ↔ property of object"), but those patterns aren't directly framed for ee-pair adjudication.

Three independent lines of evidence point the same way:
1. Both gained pairs grade as FP / borderline FP against the source text.
2. The 1 stable pair lost (`Continuity matters ↔ Primary Care Model`) is Flash-noise, not a threshold effect.
3. The 6/6/6 stability is a *prompt-length* artifact (longer prompt → Flash defaults toward rejection), not a precision win.

**Recommendation:** Keep flat 0.80 as the ee default for test_proj. Per-kind override remains useful infrastructure but **on this corpus, neither cc-tighten nor ee-loosen pays off** under v4 + Flash. Two possible follow-up directions:

- **Prompt-level work first.** The FP modes the ee 0.75–0.80 band exposes (principle ↔ regulation, policy ↔ technical control) suggest v4 needs ee-specific KEEP-SEPARATE patterns. A "v6" pass tuned for entity-level FPs could re-open the threshold-loosening question.
- **Try the embedding swap.** A different embedding model (gemini-embedding-001 or text-embedding-3-large) may push the ee true-positive cluster up into [0.80, 0.95) where the current floor catches it without exposing the FP band at all.
