# ETR ee=0.75 — 3-of-3 sweep (symmetric to cc=0.85)

> **Branch:** `feature/etr-cross-doc` @ `1ecd7f7`.
> **Pairs with:** `audits/2026-05-19_etr-per-kind-3of3-sweep.md` (cc=0.85 sweep that closes flat 0.80 as the cc default).
> **Corpus:** `test_proj`, same warm cache + post-extract graph as the cc=0.85 sweep.
> **Backend:** Gemini 2.5 Flash adjudication, `gemini-embedding-2-preview` (3072-dim), new project Gemini API key.
> **Cost:** ~$0.10–0.15 / run × 3 runs ≈ $0.40 (band is ~3× the baseline, ~9 batches each).
>
> **TL;DR:** Loosening `entityEntity` floor to 0.75 grew the ee adjudication band from ~17 → ~120 pairs/run and produced **6/6/6 approvals** (Flash became markedly more stable with the longer prompt). Stable intersection rose 3 → 4 vs baseline; 2 pairs from the new 0.75–0.80 ee band entered the stable set. Both gained pairs warrant rubric grading before any default change — surface read suggests one likely false-positive (principle↔regulation) and one borderline (paraphrase or different-scope). Net: per-kind ee loosening is *plausibly* a recall win on this corpus, but precision is unverified without the rubric.

## Method

3 `--etr-only` runs with `--adj-floor 0.80 --adj-floor-ee 0.75` against the same warm-cache project graph used for yesterday's cc=0.85 sweep. Baseline reused (`07-22-19Z`, `07-28-59Z`, `07-29-58Z` from earlier today). The 3 ee=0.75 sidecars (`08-37-14Z`, `08-40-23Z`, `08-43-09Z`) all confirm `adjudicationFloorPerKind: { entityEntity: 0.75 }` in their JSON.

## Adjudication band size

| Run | cc band | ee band | cl band | total | approved |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| base-1 | 36 | 23 | 4 | 63 | 12 |
| base-2 | 33 | 18 | 5 | 56 | 8 |
| base-3 | 34 | 11 | 6 | 51 | 7 |
| ee75-1 | 31 | 122 | 7 | 160 | 6 |
| ee75-2 | 31 | 121 | 7 | 159 | 6 |
| ee75-3 | 31 | 119 | 7 | 157 | 6 |

The ee band expanded ~7× (≈17 → ≈121 pairs). cc and cl bands held roughly constant (cc=31 vs baseline 33–36 — minor embedding-noise drift around the 0.80 boundary).

Notable: per-run approvals are **dramatically more stable** under ee=0.75 (6/6/6) than under baseline (12/8/7) or cc=0.85 (3/1/1). Hypothesis: a longer adjudication prompt (more pairs per batch) shifts Flash's default toward conservative rejection, which suppresses the per-run noise that drives the baseline's 12 → 7 swing. Cost is the lower average approval count — but the stable set actually grew.

**Δ:**
- Shared: 2 (the high-confidence ee + the regulatory-subset cc).
- Lost: 1 — `Continuity matters ↔ Primary Care Model` (cl, 0.817). Same pair that flapped out of the cc=0.85 stable set — its band membership was unchanged (cl floor stayed at 0.80), so the loss is Flash noise sensitivity, not a structural threshold effect. The pair was already on the edge of stability.
- **Gained: 2**, both in the newly-accessible 0.75–0.80 ee band:
  - `Data serves the patient ↔ Patient Rights Under HIPAA` (0.767)
  - `Workforce Access ↔ additional access controls` (0.766)

## Surface read on the gained pairs (pre-rubric)

1. **`Data serves the patient ↔ Patient Rights Under HIPAA` (0.767).** "Data serves the patient" reads as a brand value or operational principle; "Patient Rights Under HIPAA" is a specific regulatory framework. Different scopes. Under v4 this likely fires the "parallel regimes that share a noun" KEEP-SEPARATE pattern. **Probable false-positive** — but Flash approved it 3/3 with the longer prompt, suggesting Flash sees a relationship the prompt doesn't explicitly handle.
2. **`Workforce Access ↔ additional access controls` (0.766).** Borderline. "Workforce Access" is plausibly the umbrella; "additional access controls" is one layer of that umbrella. Could be a leaf-of-catalog FP, or a legitimate paraphrase ("workforce access controls" being the topic with the "additional" layer named separately). Needs the source PDFs to grade.

Both gains need the test_proj rubric to grade precision. The v3-grounded rubric in `audits/2026-05-18_rubric-v3-vitacare.md` would need extension to cover these pairs since they sit in the new ee 0.75–0.80 band that wasn't in scope before.

## Verdict (provisional, pending rubric)

Per-kind ee loosening shows **real candidate-extraction**: 2 new stable approvals in the 0.75–0.80 band that flat 0.80 would have excluded entirely. The stable count moved from 3 → 4 with one Flash-noise loss. That's stronger evidence than the cc=0.85 sweep produced — no genuine recall loss from threshold tightening, and 2 new candidates surfaced.

But "candidates surfaced" ≠ "good merges." Without rubric grading, I can't tell whether these gains are precision wins or FPs that v4's prompt isn't filtering. The surface read suggests at least one likely FP.

**Recommendation:** Do not promote ee=0.75 as a default yet. Grade the 2 gained pairs against the test_proj source PDFs; if both rubric-MERGE, ee=0.75 is a clean win for this corpus. If one is FP, the per-kind primitive is the right shape but the prompt needs an anti-pattern for the regression mode the new band exposes.
